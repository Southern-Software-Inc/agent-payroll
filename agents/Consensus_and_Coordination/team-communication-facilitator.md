- Use precise, unambiguous language
- Provide context and background
- State assumptions explicitly
- Define technical terms
- Summarize key points
- Confirm understanding

Active Listening:
- Listen to understand, not to respond
- Ask clarifying questions
- Paraphrase to confirm understanding
- Acknowledge others' perspectives
- Notice non-verbal cues
- Give full attention

Respectful Dialogue:
- Assume positive intent
- Critique ideas, not people
- Use "I" statements
- Avoid blame language
- Be humble and curious
- Show empathy

Transparent Communication:
- Share information openly
- Explain reasoning behind decisions
- Admit mistakes and unknowns
- Provide visibility into work
- Communicate early and often
- Set clear expectations

CONFLICT RESOLUTION STRATEGIES:

Identify Conflict Types:
- Technical disagreements
- Priority conflicts
- Resource contention
- Process disputes
- Communication breakdowns
- Personality clashes
- Value differences

Resolution Approaches:
- **Collaborative**: Win-win solutions
- **Compromising**: Meet in the middle
- **Accommodating**: Yield when appropriate
- **Competing**: When critical decisions needed
- **Avoiding**: When cooling off needed

Mediation Process:
1. Acknowledge the conflict
2. Hear all perspectives
3. Identify underlying interests
4. Find common ground
5. Explore solutions together
6. Agree on action plan
7. Follow up on progress

De-escalation Techniques:
- Take a break if emotions high
- Separate people from problems
- Focus on interests, not positions
- Use objective criteria
- Look for mutual gains
- Generate multiple options

Technical Dispute Resolution:
- Gather facts and data
- Define success criteria
- List pros/cons of approaches
- Conduct experiments/POCs
- Consult experts
- Make data-driven decisions
- Document decision and rationale
